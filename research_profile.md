## Researcher Background

- PhD student working at the intersection of wireless communications and artificial intelligence.
- Primary interest: AI-assisted acquisition, reconstruction, compression, feedback, and reasoning over channel state information (CSI) for future wireless networks.
- Prefer papers that connect rigorous wireless communication problems with modern AI methods, especially when the method is practical under limited pilots, varying SNRs, changing pilot densities, or constrained model complexity.

## Core Research Direction

- Channel estimation, CSI acquisition, channel reconstruction, and channel extrapolation for MIMO, massive MIMO, OFDM, mmWave, THz, RIS-assisted, cell-free, and 6G wireless systems.
- CSI feedback, CSI compression, codebook learning, limited-feedback MIMO, implicit/explicit CSI representation, and neural compression for high-dimensional channel matrices.
- Channel knowledge graphs and structured wireless environment knowledge: map/location-aware CSI modeling, geometry-aware channel prediction, radio environment maps, semantic channel representation, and knowledge-guided wireless AI.
- AI for wireless physical layer problems, including deep learning, Transformers, masked autoencoders, diffusion models, generative models, graph neural networks, attention mechanisms, foundation models, and lightweight neural architectures.

## High Priority Topics

- Channel estimation or channel reconstruction from sparse, noisy, partial, or low-resolution pilot observations.
- CSI acquisition with reduced pilot overhead, adaptive pilot density, pilot design, compressive sensing, sparse recovery, and model robustness across SNR or channel conditions.
- Location-aided, map-aided, geometry-aided, or environment-aware channel estimation/reconstruction; cross-modal fusion between channel observations and auxiliary information such as user location, sensing data, maps, or radio environment knowledge.
- Masked autoencoders, self-supervised learning, representation learning, Transformers, tokenization of channel matrices, and cross-modal attention for wireless channels.
- Diffusion models, DDPM/DDIM, score-based generative models, and generative priors for channel estimation, denoising, reconstruction, extrapolation, or CSI feedback.
- RIS-assisted and mmWave channel estimation, including direct/cascaded channel estimation, RIS phase design, low-SNR robustness, and lightweight neural estimators.
- CSI feedback and compression using autoencoders, vector quantization, entropy models, learned codebooks, neural quantization, or robust feedback under limited bandwidth.
- Channel knowledge graphs, wireless knowledge graphs, graph-based channel reasoning, retrieval-augmented wireless AI, and integration of LLMs/foundation models with structured wireless-domain knowledge.
- Practicality-oriented AI methods: generalization across pilot densities/SNRs/scenarios, parameter-efficient architectures, low-complexity inference, online adaptation, transfer learning, and deployment-aware design.

## Lower Priority / Exclusions

- Pure computer vision, NLP, recommender systems, robotics, bio/medical AI, or generic machine learning papers with no clear wireless communication or CSI connection.
- Network-layer, MAC-layer, edge/cloud computing, IoT application, security, blockchain, or semantic communication papers unless they directly involve CSI acquisition, channel modeling, or physical-layer AI.
- Classical communication theory papers that do not involve channel estimation, CSI feedback/compression, propagation modeling, or AI-assisted physical-layer design.
- Pure hardware/circuit/antenna design papers unless they have a clear impact on CSI acquisition, channel reconstruction, RIS/mmWave channel estimation, or AI-based channel modeling.

## Scoring Guide

- 5: Directly useful for current research; combines wireless CSI/channel problems with AI methods such as diffusion models, masked autoencoders, Transformers, GNNs, knowledge graphs, or neural compression. Should be read today.
- 4: Clearly related to channel estimation, CSI acquisition, CSI feedback/compression, channel reconstruction, RIS/mmWave/MIMO channels, or channel knowledge modeling, even if the AI component is less central.
- 3: Adjacent background or a potentially transferable AI/wireless method, such as general generative modeling, self-supervised learning, pilot optimization, or radio-map learning with partial CSI relevance.
- 2: Same broad wireless/AI area but weak connection to CSI, channel estimation, feedback, compression, or channel knowledge.
- 1: Mostly unrelated to the stated research profile.
- 0: Unrelated or impossible to judge from the abstract.
